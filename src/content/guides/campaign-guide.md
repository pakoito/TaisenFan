# Sangokushi Taisen Ten - Campaign Guide

Welcome to the Strategy Chapter (戦略ノ章), the campaign mode of Sangokushi Taisen Ten. This guide will take you through all six chapters plus the Warring States mode, helping you overcome each challenge.

Whether you're new to the Three Kingdoms or an experienced strategy gamer, this guide will help you understand the game's mechanics and overcome each challenge.

> **Terminology Note:** This guide uses descriptive English terms for readability. In the game's UI: cavalry = Horse, archers = Bow, spearmen = Spear, siege units = Ram. Stats are POW (attack), INT (intelligence), HP (health), MP (morale/skill meter), SPD (speed). Character Skills are called "Skills" and Sage special abilities are called "Abilities."

---

## Before You Begin: Key Mechanics

Before diving into campaign, understand these important mechanics that will shape your strategy.

### HP Recovery is Slow

A unit at half HP can take nearly the entire battle to naturally recover. Every point of damage matters.

> **Tip:** Recovery skills are not optional, they're mandatory. Bring at least one healer.

### Castle Revival System

Units don't revive on a timer wherever they fell. They must physically return to your castle to revive. If a unit dies deep in enemy territory, it takes ages to return and get back into the fight.

> **Tip:** Don't overextend. Dying far from home is a big punishment.

### Siege Units Revive at Full HP

Siege units (攻城兵) come back at 100% HP when they revive. This makes them incredibly sustainable attackers.

> **Tip:** Use siege units as your primary damage dealers. They're slow but steady and come back fresh every time.

### Dueling Means Instant Death

Losing a duel kills your unit outright. The duel is a rhythm-game style minigame where higher POW gives you thicker timing bars.

> **Warning:** Only duel when you have a significant POW advantage. If you're not confident, avoid it.

### Touch Controls Limit Your Options

Managing multiple cavalry units with the stylus is difficult. Even skilled players fumble when trying to control two or three at once.

> **Tip:** Stick to one or two cavalry maximum. Siege units and archers are much easier to control.

## Unit Types and How to Use Them

> **Note:** For detailed mechanics (Charge, Thrust, Counter, Run Shot), see the [Beginner's Guide](beginners-guide.md#unit-types). This section covers campaign-specific tips.

### Cavalry (騎兵)
Must be moving to charge. Arrows cancel your charge if they hit you. Very difficult to control multiples.

**Best use:** Single cavalry with infantry support. Don't try to micro multiple horses with the stylus.

### Archers (弓兵)
Set them up and let them shoot. After stopping briefly, they can use Run Shot to fire while moving. The easiest unit type to manage.

**Best use:** Defense, support fire, and as your "set and forget" damage dealers.

### Spearmen (槍兵)
Essential for stopping enemy cavalry charges. Can thrust when stationary. Spear attacks are relatively easy to perform.

**Best use:** Defensive walls, anti-cavalry duty. Long spear tactics are especially effective against the AI.

### Siege Units (攻城兵)
Slow movement but high castle damage. The full HP revival makes them broken good.

**Best use:** Primary attackers for most of the campaign. Slow speed actually makes them easier to control.

Your deck has an 8-cost limit. Units cost between 1.0 and 3.0. Here are some example compositions:

Same-faction units make your Sage Abilities significantly stronger. Running three different factions weakens your commander abilities badly.

## Understanding the Strategic Map

### How Territory Capture Works

The campaign plays out on a strategic map of China. Each turn, you choose which territory to attack or defend, assign your units, and then battles play out in real-time.

Key points:
- Capture all enemy territories (or meet specific objectives) to win
- Each victory grants card rewards
- Your unit roster grows as you win battles

### The Snowball Effect

**Winning:** Victory gives you more cards, which means a stronger army, which means easier victories. Keep winning and you'll snowball.

**Losing:** Defeats cost you units, weakening your army, making the next battle harder. If you start losing, stop attacking. Defend and rebuild.

### How Many Units to Deploy

| Situation | Minimum Force | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Defense | 3 units, ~4.5-5.0 cost | Can hold against average attacks |
| Attack | 4-5 units | Attacking with 2-3 often fails |
| Full assault | 6+ units | For tough territories with bosses |

> **Warning:** Never leave home undefended. This is the number one way people lose campaigns. Always leave at least your minimum defensive force at your home base.

### When to Attack

After successfully defending, enemies have an attack cooldown. They can't immediately threaten your home again. Use this window to counterattack aggressively.

The rhythm is: Defend, then counter, then defend, then counter.

### Territory Capture Order

**DO:** Capture weak or neutral territories first. This builds your unit roster from card rewards. A stronger army makes the harder territories easier.

**DON'T:** Attack strong enemy territories early. Always check enemy composition before committing. Look for ambush count, cavalry count, and boss units.

## Merchant System

Each chapter has merchants who sell units at three price tiers: 500, 1000, and 2000 gold. Generally:

- **500 gold:** Common units, mixed quality
- **1000 gold:** Often the best value - solid Rare cards
- **2000 gold:** Super Rares, but not always worth the price

The 1000-gold tier frequently has better quality than 2000. Don't assume more expensive is better.

> **Tip:** If you select starting units that would normally appear in a merchant's inventory, they count as "already owned" and won't appear for purchase. Use this to guarantee specific cards.

## Dangerous Enemies to Watch For

### Lu Bu (呂布)
POW 15 - the highest in the game. His "Unrivaled" skill is devastating.

**How to beat him:**
1. Gang up with 3+ units simultaneously
2. Use attack buffs before engaging
3. Pin him with spearmen while others flank
4. Never, ever fight him one-on-one

### Xu Chu (許褚)
POW 10 Bow with "Tiger Strength" and Regen (recovers HP). Part of Cao Cao's army.

**Why he's dangerous:** His bow support fire while you're fighting is brutal. He recovers HP and keeps coming back.

**Counter:** Fire or water damage Skills to clear him out. Speed Tactics Horse to rush him down before he retreats to the castle.

### Lu Meng (呂蒙)
Uses "Paralyze Order" with an overpowered Wu army.

**Why he's dangerous:** POW +4, effect lasts nearly 10 counts, and the AI loves using it. Add in Wu's fence defenses and it's oppressive.

**Counter:** Speed-boosting skills to escape the paralysis zone. Don't clump your units.

### Huang Zu (黄祖)
3/8 archer with ambush, revival, and Paralysis Arrow skill. Often paired with Gan Ning.

**Why he's dangerous:** High intelligence means long skill duration. The AI uses Paralysis Arrow constantly.

**Counter:** Speed Tactics units to close the gap. Accept some Castle damage and reset if needed.

### Xiahou Dun (夏侯惇)
Uses "One Eye Roar" - a lightning attack that hits multiple targets.

**Counter:** Don't group up near him. Spread your units.

### Li Ru (李儒)
Uses "Assassin Toxin" which forces poisoned units to retreat to their Castle.

**Counter:** Don't bunch up. Bring purification or HP recovery skills.

## CPU Behavior Patterns

Understanding how the AI works helps you exploit it:

1. **After dying, CPU waits for 100% HP before attacking again.** Wounded enemies retreat and stay back until fully healed.

2. **The AI prioritizes destroying dancers (舞姫) above all else.** They'll charge through spears and poison just to kill a dancing unit. You can use this as bait.

3. **CPU prefers single-target Buffs over formation Skills.** They spam Fury Tactics (憤激戦法) and Power Tactics (強化戦法), rarely use the expensive Command Skills.

4. **CPU siege units just march forward.** Easy to predict.

5. **CPU cavalry charges at you but is easy to intercept.** They rarely dodge spear counters.

6. **Enemy initial positions are fixed.** Learn where the ambushes are and send expendable units to trigger them.

## Warring States Mode (戦乱)

**Unlocked:** After completing all 6 chapters

**Reward Card:** Guan Yu (関羽) - 3 cost Spear, 10/10, Divine Thunder of Judgment

This is the ultimate test. Everything is randomized: your starting deck, home base, surrounding enemies, merchant stock, and imperial orders.

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Start Date** | January 200 |
| **Time Limit** | 24 months |
| **Objective** | Unify China (24 territories) |

### Keys to Victory

**Restart for good conditions.** Your starting position and deck vary wildly:
- Best positions: Liaodong, Guiji, Lingling, Jianning, Xiping (only 1 adjacent territory)
- Starting cost ranges from 6 to 14 - lower is harder but scores better
- Acceptable: 1-2 adjacent territories and at least one useful character

**Imperial Orders:** Starting turn 2, you'll receive orders. Completing them gives gold, virtue, and cards. Worth doing, but don't sacrifice your defense.

**Pacing:** 24 territories in 24 turns means 1 per turn average. Since you can attack multiple territories per turn, you actually have breathing room. Don't panic.

### Scoring

- **Turn Score:** Based on completion speed. Edge positions score higher than central ones.
- **Order Bonus:** Up to 30,000 - remaining turns when completing each order add 1,000 each
- **Difficulty Bonus:** Up to 10,000 - formula is 10,000 minus (starting cost - 6.0) times 500

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using 3+ cavalry** - Too difficult to control with touch screen. Stick to 1-2 maximum.

2. **Ignoring recovery skills** - Natural HP recovery is glacially slow. Always bring healing.

3. **Triggering ambushes with your best units** - Send expendables first to scout.

4. **Fighting Lu Bu alone** - He has 15 attack. Gang up with multiple units.

5. **Mixing 3+ factions** - Severely weakens Sage Abilities. Stick to 1-2.

6. **Leaving home undefended** - The number one way to lose. Always keep defenders at your base.

7. **Bad duel decisions** - Losing means instant death. Only duel with clear advantage.

8. **Wasting Sage Abilities** - Save them for critical moments like boss fights or last-stand defense.
