## The Mindset

**COM and PVP are completely different games.** The AI behaves predictably - it follows patterns, makes the same mistakes, and rarely punishes you for overextending. Human players adapt, exploit weaknesses, and punish bad habits.

This guide focuses on the tactical thinking you need for competitive play. Even if you only play against COM, these fundamentals will make you better.

The core skill isn't mechanical - it's *thinking about the match before and during it*.

---

## Before the Match

### Reading the Opponent

When you see the enemy deck, ask yourself:
- **What does their deck want to do?** (Command deathball? Horse harassment? Dance stall?)
- **What are their key cards?** (Who carries their win condition?)
- **What's their win condition?** (Team fight? Attrition? Rush?)

If you don't know what a deck does, *you've already lost the mental game*. After the match, try building and playing that deck yourself. You'll understand its strengths and weaknesses far better than any guide can teach.

### Planning Your Response

Once you understand their deck:
- Does your strategy work against theirs?
- What threats should you watch for?
- When are you strong versus when are they strong?

**Example:** You're running a Speed Order deck. They have Spear-heavy formation.
Your normal Charge strategy won't work - you'll get Countered. Shift focus to skills like Divide Ploy or Water Bane to disrupt their formation before engaging.

### Predicting Their Setup

Based on terrain and their deck composition:
- Where will they position at game start?
- What Ability will they likely bring?
- Do they have Ambushes? Where will they hide?

If total POW or unit types are mismatched, the opening becomes clearer. If you're running Speed deck and they have no Spears with Paralyze Arrow, you'll have the early advantage.

## Unit Tactics

### Horse

**Role:** Flanking, harassment, cleanup, damage

The only unit type viable as a mono-type deck. Highest mobility means you control engagement timing.

**High-POW Horse**
- Core damage dealers for your army
- Use both Charge AND melee
- You're a priority target - protect from enemy skills
- Can bait enemy abilities by threatening then retreating

**Low-POW Horse**
- Harassment and utility
- Ambush detection (send them through suspected hiding spots)
- Flank sieges when enemy defends center
- Expendable, but don't throw them away for nothing

**Key Principles**
- **Keep moving** - stationary Horse is wasted Horse
- **Always watch for Spears** - one Counter can end your unit
- **High survival rate** - if no enemy skills threaten you, you can always escape
- Charge builds when moving at certain speed - you get flash when ready

---

### Spear

**Role:** Anti-Horse, support, anchor

Every non-Horse deck needs at least one Spear. Without Spears, enemy Horse will Charge freely.

**High-POW Spear**
- Combat anchor - apply pressure while allies support
- Lead the advance into enemy territory
- Siege threat that demands response

**Low-POW Spear**
- Support only - stay behind allies
- **Never melee alone** - you'll melt instantly
- Overlap Thrust zones with allies for layered defense
- Very skill-dependent - good Spear play shows mastery

**Key Principles**
- **Stay center** for best coverage against flanking Horse
- **Once you advance, retreat is hard** - commit wisely
- Recruitment or Revival traits are valuable on Spears
- Stack Thrust zones - even low-POW Spears contribute this way

---

### Bow

**Role:** Ranged support, defense

Strong on offense (supporting your push), fragile when defending your own castle.

**Key Principles**
- **Strong vs Spear** - they can't close distance easily
- **Weak vs Horse** - Charges ignore your damage; they close too fast
- **Need protection** - always have allies between you and threats
- Target non-charging, low-POW enemies first
- Halves enemy siege rate when hitting attackers at your castle

**Note:** Changing bow target requires you to stop shooting first. Once you lock onto an enemy, you keep shooting them until you stop and reposition. Plan your targeting carefully.

---

### Sword (Infantry)

**Role:** Melee specialist, Spear counter

No special attack means you *must* melee to contribute. This is actually an advantage in specific situations.

**Key Principles**
- **Force melee to disable enemy specials** - Spear Thrust disappears in melee
- **Especially effective vs Spears** - cancels their zone, negates their advantage
- **Second fastest unit** - catches Spears and Bows easily
- Good for flank sieges when enemy defends center
- Often have powerful skills to compensate for no special attack

---

### Ram (Siege Unit)

**Role:** Castle damage specialist

The slowest unit with weakest combat stats, but massive siege damage (roughly 3x normal units).

**Key Principles**
- **Requires ability and skill coordination** - build your whole strategy around protecting it
- **Plan for the push** - if one reaches the castle, it's a major threat
- Takes slightly less melee damage and deals almost none
- Touch damage on contact is still decent
- Most decks don't use Ram - but those that do build entirely around it

## Match Flow

### Opening Phase

**~0-20C (first 20 counts)**

MP is too low for most skills. This phase is determined by:
- Raw POW advantage
- Ambush positions and reveals
- Initial formation and unit matchups

**Play to your deck's concept:**
- Aggressive decks: Push for early siege damage
- Defensive decks: Survive, build MP, wait for your power spike
- Ambush decks: Position carefully, deny enemy scouts

The side with higher total POW or favorable unit matchups typically controls early tempo.

---

### Mid-Game

**The core of the match**

This is where deck strategies execute:

| Deck Type | Mid-Game Goal |
|-----------|---------------|
| Command decks | Force team fights where your buff wins |
| Attrition decks | Disrupt enemy formation, pick off units |
| Superweapon decks | Protect and enable your carry unit |
| Dance decks | Activate dance, survive to build advantage |

**Results here shape the endgame.** If you take a lead, you can play safe. If you're behind, you need to take risks.

---

### Endgame

**~Final 20C**

Before committing to final push:
- **Castle HP difference** - who needs to attack?
- **MP difference** - who has resources for skills?
- **Ability availability** - who still has their Sage ability?

Sometimes the correct play is sacrificing units to secure victory. A dead unit that enables a winning siege was worth it.

## Timing Reference

These timings are essential for planning pushes and defenses.

### Siege Timing

| Action | Time |
|--------|------|
| First siege | ~2C |
| Subsequent siege (same unit) | ~4C |
| Under Bow fire | Gauge fills 2x slower |

**Tip:** After completing a siege, briefly leave the castle wall then return - this resets to fast siege speed.

### Deployment

- Deploy time: ~2C from card placement to unit appearing
- Speed buffs/debuffs affect deployment time

### Travel Times (Castle to Castle)

| Unit | Time |
|------|------|
| Horse | 4C |
| Sword | 6C |
| Bow | 6.5C |
| Spear | 8C |
| Ram | 13C |
| Ambush (hidden) | 25.5C |

Use these for endgame planning. If you need siege in 10C, Ram won't make it from your castle.

### Revival Times

| Condition | Time |
|-----------|------|
| Normal | ~15.6C (35 sec) |
| With Revival trait | ~12C (27 sec) |
| After Death Tactics | +23C added |

### Recovery Times

| Method | Time to Full HP |
|--------|-----------------|
| In castle | ~13C |
| Recruitment trait | ~13C |
| Recovery Formation Lv11 | ~10C |
| Recruitment + Formation (stacked) | ~6C |
| 8 Trigrams (4+ targets) | 9C duration, ~40% heal |

**Optimization:** Units at ~80% HP can deploy and reach full HP during travel time. Don't wait for 100% in castle.

## Managing Your Army

### Don't Lose Units Unnecessarily

Especially in Command decks, losing one unit massively reduces your team fight power.

- Avoid dying at your own castle (worst timing)
- Sometimes let enemy siege rather than lose a unit defending
- When pressed, use Charge or Thrust to deal damage without committing to melee

### Sacrificing Units Wisely

Sometimes sacrifice is correct:

**Example:** After a push, your low-cost unit is stuck at enemy castle with low HP. Enemy Bow will kill them during retreat. Options:
1. Try to retreat → Enemy keeps them alive to delay revival
2. Commit to siege → Die on your terms, start revival immediately

Option 2 is often better. You control your revival timing.

**Who to sacrifice as rearguard:**
- Slow units (Spear) - they can't escape anyway
- Low-cost units - less value lost
- Revival trait holders - they come back faster
- NOT your main carry (never sacrifice your key unit)

## Improving Your Play

### After Every Match

1. **Why did you win or lose?** (Matchup? Skill difference? Mistakes? Duel luck?)
2. **What did your opponent do well?** (Copy their good moves in training)
3. **What mistakes did you make?** (Fix one thing at a time)

### Training Methods

- **Copy opponent's good moves** - practice in training mode
- **Play the opponent's deck** - understand their perspective
- **Use decks you lose to** - learn their weaknesses firsthand

### The Key Insight

> "When you watch top players and have no questions about their decisions, you understand the game."

This is the goal. Every time you think "why did they do that?", you've found something to learn.

## Advanced Tips

### Control Techniques

- **Touch screen limitations** - you can't physically move multiple cards independently
- **Link system** - use linking to move groups; essential for coordinated pushes
- **Card angle changes** - difficult mid-movement; use cutscene pauses to reposition

### Bow Targeting

- Once you lock a target, you keep shooting them
- To change target: stop shooting, reposition, then resume
- Small movements don't trigger Run Shot - useful for micro-adjustments
- Slightly moving (card width ÷3) while firing still shoots at same timing

### Duel Mechanics

Duels trigger frequently:
- Trigger when unit centers touch (not just overlap)
- Timing bar moves at a reasonable pace
- **Prepare for Duels** - high-POW or Valor trait units benefit greatly
- **Consume Duels early** if your deck is Duel-weak

### Lag in WiFi Matches

Two types of lag:
1. **Processing lag** - screen updates slowly but predictably
2. **Teleport lag** - units jump to card position instantly

For teleport lag:
- Don't move cards far; wait for unit to settle
- Link same unit types; move as group
- Prioritize siege trading over unit combat
- Use wide-range skills (Commands, heals) over precise targeting

### Known Bugs to Exploit/Avoid

| Bug | Effect | Notes |
|-----|--------|-------|
| Wild Shots double damage | Bow target takes 2x damage | Very powerful on single target |
| Roar skills allow Spear rotation | Can rotate Spear during freeze | Partially counters the freeze |
| Multi-gate siege | Stack units on castle gate | Requires precise positioning |
| Gate suction | Gate pulls cards deep automatically | Use link to position shallowly |
