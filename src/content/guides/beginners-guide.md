# Sangokushi Taisen Ten - Beginner's Guide

Welcome to Sangokushi Taisen Ten, a real-time strategy card game set in the Three Kingdoms era of China. This guide covers everything you need to know to start playing: factions, unit types, core mechanics, and essential terminology.

> **Terminology Note:** This guide uses official English translations. Japanese equivalents are listed in the glossary at the end.

---

## What is Sangokushi Taisen?

Sangokushi Taisen is a real-time strategy card game where you command armies of legendary Three Kingdoms heroes. Each battle lasts 99 counts (about 4 minutes), and your goal is to deal more castle damage than your opponent, or destroy their castle entirely.

> **Timing Note:** 1 count is ~2.4 seconds. When skills or abilities mention durations in "counts," use this to estimate real time.

Unlike traditional card games, you physically place cards on the DS touch screen and move them around to control your units on the battlefield. Think of it as a real-time tactical battle where your cards are your troops.

### Game Features

- **Touch Controls:** Drag cards on the bottom screen to move units on the top screen
- **Single Player Modes:** DUEL for training battles, CONQUEST for the campaign
- **Card Collection:** Over 250 cards to collect through gameplay
- **Wi-Fi Battles:** Online versus mode with ranking system

### Basic Match Structure

1. Build a deck of Lords totaling 8 Cost maximum
2. Assign a Sage for their Ability
3. Place your cards on your side of the field
4. Move units to attack the enemy castle while defending your own
5. Use Skills by spending MP
6. Use your Sage's Ability once per match at a critical moment
7. When the 99-count timer expires, the side with more Castle HP wins

## Unit Types

Every Lord card has a unit type icon in the lower-left corner. There are five types, each with unique abilities and roles.

### Horse

**Speed:** Fastest | **Siege Power:** Weakest | **Special:** Charge

Horse units are all about mobility. They move faster than any other unit type and can deliver devastating Charge attacks.

**How Charge Works:**
1. Move at high speed for a certain distance without touching enemies
2. Your unit gains a white flash effect and moves 1.2× faster
3. Contact an enemy while flashing to deal massive damage

**Strengths:**
- Best mobility for flanking and quick strikes
- Charge damage ignores armor and hurts even high-POW enemies
- While flashing, incoming Bow damage is drastically reduced

**Weaknesses:**
- Lowest Siege power (about 6.5% Castle HP per attack at POW 1)
- Vulnerable to Spear Counter, which deals huge damage
- Touching a Spear's red Thrust zone while flashing triggers Counter

**Tip:** Controlling multiple Horse units is difficult. Stick to one or two maximum.

### Spear

**Speed:** Slowest | **Siege Power:** Medium | **Special:** Thrust, Counter

Spear units are the anti-Horse specialists. They move slowly but can devastate fast-moving enemies with their Counter ability.

**How Thrust Works:**
- Stop your card for about 1 second, then move it
- Your unit extends a yellow Thrust zone briefly, damaging enemies it touches
- You can chain Thrusts by stopping and moving repeatedly

**How Counter Works:**
- Your unit constantly projects a red Thrust zone in front
- If an enemy moving faster than 1.3× normal speed touches this zone, they take Counter damage
- Counter damage is enormous, often killing a full-HP Horse in one hit
- If the enemy moves faster than 2.0× normal speed, Counter damage nearly doubles

**Strengths:**
- Only unit type that can punish Horse Charges
- Medium Siege power (about 12% Castle HP per attack at POW 1)
- Thrust zone remains active as long as you're not in melee

**Weaknesses:**
- Slowest movement speed in the game
- Thrust zone disappears during melee combat
- Easy targets for Bow units due to slow speed

**Tip:** Spear units are easier to control than Horse. Long Spear Skills are particularly effective against the AI.

### Bow

**Speed:** Medium-Slow | **Siege Power:** Medium | **Special:** Ranged Attack, Run Shot

Bow units attack from a distance, damaging enemies without needing to engage in melee. They must stop briefly to start firing, but can then move while shooting using Run Shot.

**How Ranged Attack Works:**
- Stop your unit completely
- It automatically fires at enemies within range
- Targets the enemy closest to facing direction
- To change targets, rotate the card and briefly interrupt firing

**How Run Shot Works:**
- Stop your unit for about 2 seconds
- Then move it, and it continues firing while moving
- Movement speed increases to 1.2× during Run Shot
- Run Shot at 1.5× speed or faster can trigger Spear Counter

**Strengths:**
- Damage enemies without taking damage yourself
- Medium Siege power (about 12% Castle HP per attack at POW 1)
- Easiest unit type to manage: set and forget

**Weaknesses:**
- Cannot fire while moving normally
- Flashing Horse units take almost no Bow damage
- Melee combat disables ranged attacks entirely

**Tip:** Bow units are easy to manage. Set them up behind your frontline and let them work.

### Sword

**Speed:** Medium | **Siege Power:** Medium | **Special:** None

Sword units have no special abilities. They exist as a middle ground: faster than Spear and Bow, slower than Horse.

**Strengths:**
- Fast enough to catch Spear and Bow units
- Medium Siege power (about 12% Castle HP per attack at POW 1)
- Often have powerful Skills to compensate for lack of abilities

**Weaknesses:**
- No special attack or defense capabilities
- Outclassed by Horse, Spear, and Bow in their respective roles
- Considered one tier below the main three unit types

**When to Use:** Sword units typically carry powerful Skills that justify their slot. Look at the Skill first, not the unit type.

### Ram

**Speed:** Slowest | **Siege Power:** Highest | **Special:** High Defense, Weak Offense

Ram units specialize in one thing: destroying castles. Their Siege power is roughly three times higher than Spear, Bow, or Sword.

**Strengths:**
- Devastating Siege damage (about 34% Castle HP per attack at POW 1)
- Gate attacks deal about 55% Castle HP, capable of two-hit victories
- Take reduced damage from enemies (POW 1 Ram = POW 3 defense of other types)
- Ram units revive at full HP, making them incredibly sustainable

**Weaknesses:**
- Slowest unit in the game; even 2.5× speed buffs don't reach Horse speed
- Deal almost no damage in melee combat
- Easy to intercept and kill before reaching the enemy castle

**When to Use:** Ram units require protection. Pair them with Spear or Bow units to cover their approach. Their full HP revival makes them surprisingly effective in campaign mode.

## Core Mechanics

### MP

MP is the resource you spend to use Skills. It builds automatically over time.

**MP Rules:**
- Starts at 0 (or higher if you have Lords with the Charm Trait)
- Gains 1 MP every 4.125 counts (about 10 seconds)
- Maximum depends on faction composition (12 for single faction, 9 for two, 6 for three or more)
- Each Charm Trait adds 0.5 starting MP

**MP Strategy:**
- Don't let MP sit at maximum. Overflow is wasted.
- Track your MP relative to your opponent's. A 2-3 MP advantage can decide a match.
- Choosing not to use a Skill when your opponent does creates MP advantage for later.

### Skills

Every Lord has exactly one Skill. To use it:

1. Tap the Lord's card on the touch screen
2. When ready, tap the red button in the bottom-right
3. The Skill activates immediately, consuming MP

**Skill Types:**

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Command | Buffs multiple allies in range |
| Buff | Enhances the user's stats |
| Debuff | Weakens enemies |
| Damage | Deals direct HP damage to enemies |
| Dance | Immobilizes user but effect persists until death |
| Focus | Hold to charge, cannot be Denied |
| Summon | Creates ally units with set behavior |
| Deny | Counters enemy Skills within range |

**Skill Tips:**
- You can chain Skills if you have enough MP
- Some Skills can't be used consecutively due to their effects
- Use the yellow button to lock Skills, preventing accidental activation

### Abilities

Your Sage's Ability can be used once per match. It's a powerful effect that can turn the tide of battle.

**How to Use:**
1. The Ability gauge at the bottom of the screen fills over time
2. When ready, tap the blue button in the top-right
3. Full gauge = maximum effect; partial gauge = weaker effect

**Ability Types:**

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Tactics | Affects the entire battlefield |
| Formation | Affects only a preset zone |

**Common Abilities:**

| Ability | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Rally | All dead units revive faster |
| Reinforce | All units recover HP |
| Iron Chain | Enemies in zone move slower |
| Wisdom Valor | Allies in zone gain POW and INT |

### Combat Basics

**Melee Combat:**
- Units touching enemies enter melee
- Higher POW deals more damage
- All special abilities are disabled during melee
- Siege progress stops during melee

**Duel:**
- Randomly triggers when unit centers touch
- A rhythm-game style minigame decides the winner
- Higher POW gives thicker bars (easier to hit)
- Valor Trait guarantees some perfect hits
- **DS Warning:** Losing a duel kills your unit outright. Only duel with POW advantage.

**Death and Revival:**
- Units at 0 HP die and must revive
- Move the card to your castle to start revival
- Revival takes about 35 seconds (27 with Undying Trait)
- Revived units start at partial HP and must recover

### Winning the Match

**Victory Conditions (in order of priority):**
1. **Castle Fall:** Reduce enemy Castle HP to 0%
2. **HP Lead:** When time expires, side with more Castle HP wins
3. **Draw:** If Castle HP is equal, the match is a draw

**Siege Mechanics:**
- Touch the enemy castle and stop to begin siege
- A gauge fills; when full, you deal Castle damage
- Gate attacks deal roughly double damage
- Second siege attempt takes longer than the first
- You cannot siege while in melee

## Building Your First Deck

### The 8-Cost Rule

Your deck cannot exceed 8 total Cost. Cards range from 1.0 to 3.0 Cost.

**Common Compositions:**

| Cards | Example Distribution |
|-------|---------------------|
| 4 cards | 3.0 + 2.0 + 2.0 + 1.0 |
| 5 cards | 2.5 + 2.0 + 1.5 + 1.0 + 1.0 |
| 6 cards | 2.0 + 1.5 + 1.5 + 1.0 + 1.0 + 1.0 |
| 7+ cards | Many 1.0 Cost units (Swarm style) |

**Never go under 8 Cost.** Running a 7-Cost deck against an 8-Cost deck is a massive disadvantage.

### Faction Selection

Each faction has a distinct playstyle. You can identify them by card color:

| Faction | Color | Specialty |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| Wei | Red | Horse units, mobility |
| Shu | Green | Spear units, defense |
| Wu | Blue | Bow units, Fences |
| Rogue | Gray | Versatile, swarm tactics |

**Faction mixing affects your maximum MP:**

| Faction Composition | Max MP |
|---------------------|--------|
| Single faction | 12 |
| Two factions | 9 |
| Three or four factions | 6 |

Stick to one or two factions. The MP difference between 9 and 6 is significant as many powerful Skills cost 6-7 MP.

### Unit Balance

A balanced deck typically includes:

1. **At least one Spear:** Essential for stopping enemy Horse Charges
2. **One or two main damage dealers:** High POW units with good Skills
3. **Support units:** Low-Cost Lords with useful Skills or Traits
4. **Consider your Sage:** Match faction for better Ability effects

### Beginner Deck Tips

- **Spear first:** Always include at least one Spear. Without Counter, you're helpless against Horse-heavy decks.
- **Limit Horse units:** Stick to 1-2 Horse maximum due to touch control limitations.
- **Try Ram units:** They revive at full HP, making them surprisingly strong.
- **Watch your Skills:** A 1-Cost Lord with a great Skill beats a 1.5-Cost Lord with a bad one.

## Gameplay Tips

### Control Tips

- **Stick to 1-2 Horse units.** Managing multiple fast-moving units with the stylus is difficult.
- **Bow units are your friend.** Set them up and let them fire. Minimal management required.
- **Spear attacks are forgiving.** The timing for Thrust chains is lenient.

### Recovery is Slow

HP recovery is slow. A unit at half HP can take nearly an entire battle to recover naturally.

**What this means:**
- Healing Skills and Abilities are essential, not optional
- Don't trade damage carelessly; every hit matters
- Units that die far from your castle take ages to return and revive

### Ram Revival

Ram units revive at full HP, unlike other unit types. This makes them surprisingly sustainable attackers.

### CPU Behavior

The AI is reasonably smart:
- Enemies will intercept your siege attempts
- They use hammer and anvil tactics
- They sometimes whiff Skills, but not often
- They won't leave units stuck in pointless positions

### Menu Notes

- **Tutorials unlock cards.** Complete all tutorials in DUEL mode to receive 6 free cards.
- **Wi-Fi improves drops.** Higher rarity cards appear more often in online battles.
- **Sages only level in DUEL.** Campaign and Wi-Fi battles don't train your Sages.
